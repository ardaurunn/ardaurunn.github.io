ABOUT ME

I am a graduate in Geomatics Engineering and currently a MSc student in the Geographical Information Technologies program at Istanbul Technical University.

My research interests center around geospatial data which can be representative as both dynamic and static map related to the interdiciplinary scientific fields such as infrastructure, construction, archaeology, geography, sociology, history etc.

My bachelor thesis is about generating a dynamic web map-based library of the Battlefield Archaeology using Leaflet and PostGIS. Besides that, I am an enthusiast of cartographic design and 3D modelling. If you want to get a glimpse of what I do, see Research Highlights below.

CARTOGRAPHIC DESIGNS FOR THE MALAZGIRT PROJECT

To visualize the battlefield more precise, expository and aspectable image, cartographic design is handled during this project.

WEB-MAP OF MALAZGIRT BATTLEFIELD ARCHAEOLOGY

Dynamic maps are perfect for making the work more understandable. In this study, the distribution and attribute of the archaeological finds in the Manzikert battlefield were dealed with using PostGIS and the Leaflet library.

NETWORK AND SUITABILITY ANALYSIS OF FIRE STATIONS

It is very crucial for the fire trucks to reach the incident on time. This study introduces a method for detecting the arrival time of the fire trucks within the district Cigli, Izmir. Including various parameters such as sloping roads, population, traffic density by time, incident locations etc. to perform network and suitability analysis. By this means, it was opined about where the new fire station should be built.

3D MODELS OF PERGAMON ANCIENT CITY

Modeling the terrain of the ancient city of Pergamon and the Trajan Temple with real dimension in Blender.

ALULA PROJECT

Before starting the survey, the GIS database used during the project was designed in QGIS with SQL. Geospatial data taken from the field was processed in the QGIS environment and made ready to be queried according to the attribute table. Also, Geographic analyzes such as 'watershed', 'accumulation' and 'water erosion model' relevant to the survey area were done for reporting.

GEOSPATIAL ANALYSIS FOR DR.OĞUZHAN KARAÇETİN'S PHD DISSERTATION

Viticulture dates back to ancient times, and thanks to them, various economic models of ancient cities can be generated. In this study, the suitability analysis of viticulture areas with certain topographic parameters was performed and an economic model was created according to these data.
